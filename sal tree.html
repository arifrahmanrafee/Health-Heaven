Sal Tree

Scientic name : Shorea robusta

S. robusta of the Dipterocarpaceae family and is commonly known as ‘sal tree’ and locally known as ‘shaal’, in Bangladesh. This plant contains different active constituents like mangiferonic acid, uvaol, ursolic acid, and amyrin, asiatic acid, tri, and tetrehydrox-yursenoic acid. Ointments prepared from S. robusta are traditionally used in curing different ailments such as ulcers, wounds, hemorrhoids, burns, dermatitis, pain, swelling, ear problems, and eye problems. The resin or oleoresin (gum) of the plant is used in curing diarrhea, gonorrhea, and dysentery [236]. Rats received S. robusta resin at doses of 150 and 300 mg/kg for treating ethanol and pyloric ligation-induced gastric ulcer, prevented gastric mucosal damage, decreased gastric juice volume, total acidity, and pepsin secretion.

Health Benefits

  • The bark and resin of the sal tree are used to treat various gastrointestinal issues such as diarrhea, dysentery, and ulcers. They help in soothing the digestive tract and improving gut health.
  • Various parts of the sal tree, including the bark, leaves, and resin, are used in traditional medicine. They are employed in treating a range of ailments, from skin diseases and wounds to gastrointestinal and respiratory conditions.
  • Sal tree extracts are used in traditional medicine to treat various skin conditions, including eczema, psoriasis, and rashes. The antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties help soothe and heal the skin.
  • Sal tree bark is used in traditional oral care practices. It helps in treating gum diseases, mouth ulcers, and preventing tooth decay due to its antimicrobial properties.

Disease Prevention

  • The antimicrobial properties of Sal tree extracts can help prevent and treat various infections, including skin infections, respiratory infections, and gastrointestinal infections.
  • Traditional uses of Sal tree bark and extracts include managing diabetes. Some studies indicate that Sal tree extracts may help regulate blood sugar levels and improve insulin sensitivity, although more research is needed.
  • In Ayurveda, the Sal tree is used to balance the Kapha and Pitta doshas. It is included in various formulations for treating respiratory issues, skin conditions, and digestive disorders.
  • Decoctions and infusions made from Sal tree bark are used for treating diarrhea, dysentery, and other gastrointestinal issues. They are also used to manage fevers and respiratory conditions.
