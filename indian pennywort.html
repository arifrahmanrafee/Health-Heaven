Indian Pennywort

Scientic name : Mimosa pudica

C. asiatica of the Umbelliferae family is locally known as ‘thankunipata’ in Bangladesh and as ‘Indian pennywort’ worldwide. It originates from the wetlands of Asia . The paste of the leaves of C. asiatica is locally used for ulcers and different gastric disorders. Traditionally, this plant has been used to treat diarrhea, rheumatism, skin diseases, syphilis, and in ammation. Several main components i.e., madecassoside, madecassic acid, asiaticoside, and asiatic acid, present in C. asiatica were extensively studied for therapeutic purposes including ulcers and in ammatory diseases. Asiaticoside (molecular formula: C48H78O19) (Figure 1), a major active constituent of C. asiatica, plays an important role in healing gastric ulcers. Asiaticoside is a glycoside compound that belongs to the triterpenoid group. Acetic acid-induced gastric ulcers in rats were reduced with a lower lesion score, minimized ulcer size, and reduced or absent leucocytes in ltration and submucosal edema, when the plant extract was administered orally. Rats pre-treated with leaf extract exhibited comparatively better protection of the gastric mucosa.

Health Benefits

  • Mimosa pudica has been found to possess antimicrobial properties, making it effective against a variety of pathogens, including bacteria and fungi. This makes it useful in treating infections and preventing their spread.
  • Mimosa pudica is known for its antidiarrheal properties. It helps in controlling and treating diarrhea by reducing the motility of the gastrointestinal tract and promoting the absorption of water.
  • The plant has been shown to possess antiulcer properties, making it useful in preventing and treating gastric ulcers. It works by protecting the gastric mucosa and reducing acidity.
  • The plant has analgesic properties that help in relieving pain. It can be used to manage headaches, muscle pain, and other types of discomfort.
  • Mimosa pudica is traditionally used to stop bleeding. It helps in the rapid clotting of blood, making it useful in treating minor cuts and wounds.

Disease Prevention

  • Due to its antimicrobial properties, Mimosa pudica can help in preventing and treating various infectious diseases by inhibiting the growth of harmful microorganisms.
  • The plant’s antidiarrheal and antiulcer properties make it effective in preventing and managing gastrointestinal disorders, including diarrhea and ulcers.
  • The antioxidant properties of Mimosa pudica help in preventing diseases related to oxidative stress, such as cancer, cardiovascular diseases, and neurodegenerative disorders.
