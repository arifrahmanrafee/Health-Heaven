Apple of Sodom

Scientic name : Calotropis procera

C. procera of the Asclepiadaecae family is popularly termed ‘akanda’ in Bangladesh and ‘milkweed’ worldwide. The leaf, flower, flower bud, latex, root bark, and root of this plant contain different bioactive compounds such as triterpenoids, lupeol, flavonoids, flavanols, glycosides, resins, cardenolides, mudarine, anthocyanins, - amyrin, amyrin, calactin, calotropin, and -sitosterol. Bioactive compounds from C. procera show protective properties against different diseases. Traditionally, the latex is used in reducing toothache, in the treatment of vertigo, paralysis, hair loss, baldness, and rheumatoid/joint swellings. In addition, the leaves have therapeutic values to reduce swelling as well as to treat joint pain. The bark is used in the treatment of eczema, leprosy, and elephantiasis treatment.

Health Benefits

  • Extracts from the plant have shown antimicrobial effects against a variety of pathogens, including bacteria, fungi, and viruses. This makes it useful for treating infections and preventing microbial growth.
  • Calotropis procera has been traditionally used to promote wound healing. Its latex is applied to cuts, wounds, and ulcers to aid in healing and prevent infection.
  • Some studies suggest that extracts from Calotropis procera have cytotoxic effects on cancer cells, indicating potential use in cancer treatment. However, more research is needed in this area.
  • The plant has been used to treat digestive issues such as constipation, indigestion, and stomach ulcers. It can help improve digestion and relieve gastrointestinal discomfort.
  • The plant has been shown to have hypoglycemic effects, which can help lower blood sugar levels and manage diabetes. Its extracts can improve insulin sensitivity and glucose metabolism.

Disease Prevention

  • Some compounds in Calotropis procera have been studied for their potential to lower blood pressure and improve heart health. They may help in reducing the risk of cardiovascular diseases.
  • The plant is rich in antioxidants, which can help protect the body from oxidative stress and reduce the risk of chronic diseases, including heart disease and cancer.
  • Calotropis procera can boost the immune system, helping the body to fight off infections and diseases more effectively.

Considerations and Precautions

While Calotropis procera has various health benefits, it is crucial to use it with caution due to its potential toxicity. Here are some important considerations:

  • Toxicity: The latex and other parts of Calotropis procera can be toxic if ingested or used improperly. Symptoms of toxicity include nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and potentially severe reactions like cardiac issues.
  • Allergic Reactions: Some individuals may have allergic reactions to the plant. It is advisable to perform a patch test before applying it to the skin.
  • Pregnancy and Breastfeeding: Pregnant and breastfeeding women should avoid using Calotropis procera due to its potential toxicity and effects on the fetus or infant.
