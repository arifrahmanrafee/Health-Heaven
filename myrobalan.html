Myrobalan

Scientic name : Terminalia chebula

T. chebula, of the Combretaceae family, is commonly known as ‘chebulic myrobalan’. In Bangladesh and India, it is well known as ‘haritoki’. This plant contains diverse bioactive chemical compounds such as chebulic acid, sorbitol, chebulinic acid, tannic acid, chebulagic acid, lucilage, gallic acid, tannin, corilagin, fixed oils, ellagic acid, resin, flavonoids, fructose, amino acids, and sterols. T. chebula mixed with triphala and sindhu salt, is used in treating ulcers and ulcerated wounds. The fruit helps in the treatment of blood dysentery and stomachache. The maceration of the bark of T. chebula, in addition to other medicinal plants, helps in waist pain and pain in bones.

Health Benefits

  • Haritaki is renowned for its digestive benefits. It helps stimulate digestion, alleviate constipation, and promote regular bowel movements. It also aids in treating indigestion, bloating, and gas.
  • Haritaki acts as a natural detoxifier, helping to cleanse the body of toxins. It supports liver health and promotes the elimination of waste products from the body.
  • Haritaki helps in reducing cholesterol levels and regulating blood pressure, thereby supporting cardiovascular health. Its antioxidant properties also contribute to heart health.
  • Haritaki is believed to enhance cognitive function and memory. It is used in traditional medicine to improve mental clarity and focus.

Disease Prevention

  • The antimicrobial properties of Haritaki make it effective in preventing and treating various infections, including bacterial, viral, and fungal infections
  • By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Haritaki can help prevent chronic diseases such as heart disease, neurodegenerative disorders, and certain cancers.
  • Haritaki is used in traditional oral care practices to treat gum diseases, mouth ulcers, and dental caries. It helps maintain oral hygiene and health.
