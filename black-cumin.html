Black Cumin

Scientic name : Nigella sativa

Belongs to Ranunculaceae family known as black cumin or kalonji, which is used as a spice in many cuisine. Its seed has been used to make medicine for thousands of year. Black seed might have effects in the body that help boost the immune system, fight cancer, prevent pregnancy, reduce swelling, and lessen allergic reactions by acting as an antihistamine. People commonly use black seed for asthma, hay fever, diabetes, high blood pressure, eczema, weight loss, menstrual cramps, and many other conditions, but there is no good scientific evidence to support many of these uses. There is also no good evidence to support using black seed for COVID-19.

Health Benefits

  • Black cumin seeds are a good source of essential fatty acids, vitamins (such as vitamin A, vitamin C, and several B vitamins), and minerals (including calcium, potassium, and iron).
  • They contain powerful antioxidants, such as thymoquinone, carvacrol, t-anethole, and 4-terpineol, which help protect the body from oxidative stress and damage caused by free radicals.
  • Regular consumption of black cumin seeds can enhance immune function, helping the body to better fight infections and diseases.
  • The seeds have been traditionally used to treat respiratory conditions like asthma, bronchitis, and allergies due to their bronchodilator and anti-inflammatory effects.

Disease Prevention

  • Studies have shown that thymoquinone in black cumin seeds can inhibit the growth of various types of cancer cells, including breast, prostate, and colon cancer cells. Its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties contribute to its cancer-fighting potential.
  • Black cumin seeds can help regulate blood sugar levels and improve insulin sensitivity, making them beneficial for managing type 2 diabetes. They can also help prevent complications related to diabetes.
  • These seeds can help lower blood pressure, reduce cholesterol levels, and improve overall heart health. Their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects contribute to protecting the cardiovascular system.
  • Black cumin seeds have hepatoprotective properties, meaning they can help protect the liver from damage and improve its function. This is particularly beneficial for detoxification processes in the body.
