Drumstick Tree

Scientic name : Moringa oleifera

M. oleifera of the Moringaceae family is locally known as ‘shajna’ in Bangladesh and as ‘drum-stick tree’ worldwide. This plant contains active constituents like alkaloids, beta carotene, tannins, beta sitosterol, zeatin, quercetin, flavonoids, kaempferom, protein, vitamins, minerals, amino acids, phenolic acids, natural sugars, phytosterols, saponin, and terpenoids. Quercetin, a flavonoid compound in M. oleifera, showed strong anti-inflammatory activity. The leaf of M. oleifera helps in the treatment of ulcers, indigestion, asthma, and sinus pain. The ethanolic root extract significantly decreased ulcer index, total acidity, and enhanced gastric pH at both doses of 350 and 500 mg/kg. The extract also has anti-secretory and cytoprotective potentiality. The alcoholic leaf extract also resulted in the reduction of acid pepsin secretion and ulcer lesion. The acetone, methanol, chloroform, and petroleum ether extracts of the leaves and fruits of M. oleifera were investigated on both gastric and duodenal ulcers using acetic acid, pylorus ligation, indomethacin, ethanol, and cold-restraint stressinduced gastric ulcer and cysteamine-induced duodenal ulcer model. The methanol and acetone extract of the leaves exerted significant gastric antisecretory and gastric cytoprotective effects in pylorus-ligated rats and both ethanol- and indomethacininduced gastric ulcers, respectively. Compared to the insignificant antiulcer effect of fruit, the leaf extract reduced both cysteamine-induced duodenal ulcers and the stress-induced gastric ulcers remarkably.

Health Benefits

  • Moringa can help lower cholesterol levels and regulate blood pressure, reducing the risk of heart disease. Its antioxidant properties also contribute to cardiovascular health.
  • Moringa has been shown to help lower blood sugar levels and improve insulin sensitivity, making it beneficial for people with diabetes or those at risk of developing diabetes.
  • The high levels of vitamins A and C in moringa leaves help boost the immune system, enhancing the body's ability to fight off infections and diseases.
  • The antioxidants and essential nutrients in moringa support brain health and cognitive function. Moringa also contains compounds that may help combat neurodegenerative diseases.
  • Moringa is rich in calcium and phosphorus, which are essential for maintaining healthy bones and preventing conditions like osteoporosis.
  • Moringa oil is used in skincare products due to its moisturizing and antioxidant properties. It helps maintain skin health, reduce inflammation, and promote wound healing. The protein and vitamins in moringa also support healthy hair growth.

Disease Prevention

  • Moringa helps protect the liver from damage caused by toxins and supports liver function. Its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties contribute to liver health.
  • Moringa has antimicrobial properties that can help combat bacterial, viral, and fungal infections. This makes it useful in treating and preventing infections.
  • Studies have shown that moringa can help reduce bad cholesterol levels (LDL) while increasing good cholesterol levels (HDL), which is beneficial for heart health.
