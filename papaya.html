Papaya

Scientic name : Carica papaya

C. papaya of the Caricaceae family is commonly known as ‘papaya’. C. papaya contains diverse active constituents like enzymes (e.g., papain, chemopapain, chymopapain, peptidase, lysosome, and myrosine), proteins, fats (e.g., myristic, palmitic, stearic, and linoleic), carbohydrates (e.g., glucose, fructose, galactose, and xylitol), minerals, vitamins, volatile compounds, alkaloids (e.g., carpain, pseudocarpain, choline, and caproside), glycosides, and carotenoids. The most common and signi cant constituent, papain, is a papaya proteinase I (cysteine protease) enzyme which has several therapeutic effects particularly in in ammation and gastrointestinal disorders. Traditionally, the decoctions of leaves and dried owers were used as anti-anemic agents, blood purifiers, and in several diseases. Fruits are used to treat impotence and ulcer. The crude latex decoction is used to treat anthelmintic, dyspepsia, burns pain, bleeding hemorrhoids, stomachic, and diarrhea.

Health Benefits

  • Papaya is packed with essential nutrients, including vitamins (particularly vitamin C, vitamin A, and folate), minerals (such as potassium, magnesium, and calcium), and dietary fiber.
  • Papaya contains the enzyme papain, which aids in digestion by breaking down proteins. This can help alleviate symptoms of indigestion, bloating, and constipation.
  • The fruit has anti-inflammatory effects due to its high levels of antioxidants, including vitamin C, flavonoids, and beta-carotene. These compounds help reduce inflammation and prevent inflammatory diseases.
  • The antioxidants, fiber, and potassium in papaya contribute to cardiovascular health by helping to lower blood pressure, reduce LDL cholesterol levels, and improve overall heart function.
  • Papaya promotes healthy skin by providing essential nutrients and antioxidants that help reduce signs of aging, such as wrinkles and sagging skin. Papain also helps in the removal of dead skin cells, promoting a clear and radiant complexion.
  • Papaya is a good source of vitamin A and beta-carotene, which are essential for maintaining healthy vision and preventing age-related macular degeneration and other eye disorders.

Disease Prevention

  • Papaya has a low glycemic index and is rich in fiber, which helps regulate blood sugar levels. It can be beneficial for people with diabetes by preventing spikes in blood sugar and improving insulin sensitivity.
  • The antioxidants and vitamins in papaya can help support kidney function and reduce the risk of kidney disease. Papaya seeds, in particular, have been shown to have protective effects against kidney damage.
  • The seeds of papaya have been used traditionally to treat intestinal parasites and infections. They also have antimicrobial properties and can support digestive health
  • Papaya leaf extract is used to increase platelet count in patients with dengue fever and other conditions. It also has anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting properties.
