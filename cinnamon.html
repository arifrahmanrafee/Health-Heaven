Cinnamon

Scientic name : Cinnamomum cassia

C. cassia of the Lauraceae family is commonly known as ‘daruchini’ in Bangladesh and as ‘cinnamon’ world-wide. The bark of cinnamon contains di erent active constituents such as terpenoids, phenylpropanoids, glycosides, cinnamaldehyde, cinnamic acid, cinnamate, and numerous essential oils (e.g., trans-cinnamal-dehyde, cinnamyl acetate, eugenol, L-borneol), etc. The C. cassia plant is traditionally used as a spice and cures dental problems, prevents colon cancer, and acts as a coagulant to prevent bleeding [169]. It performs di erent anti-in ammatory activities.Cinnamaldehyde (molecular formula: C9H8O) (Figure 1), the most abundant phytocomponents of C. cassia, was active as an anti-in ammatory agent in gastric in ammation. This aromatic aldehyde compound inhibited IL-8 secretion/expression and the nuclear factor kappa B (NF-κB) pathway to treat Helicobacter pylori-induced gastric in ammation.

Health Benefits

  • Cinnamon has been shown to improve insulin sensitivity and lower blood sugar levels. It can slow the breakdown of carbohydrates in the digestive tract, reducing the rise in blood sugar after meals, making it beneficial for people with type 2 diabetes.
  • Cinnamon can help reduce risk factors for heart disease, including high cholesterol, high triglycerides, and high blood pressure. Its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties also contribute to heart health.
  • Cinnamon can aid in digestion by increasing gastrointestinal motility and relieving symptoms of indigestion, bloating, and gas. It may also help prevent nausea and vomiting.
  • Compounds in cinnamon, such as cinnamaldehyde and epicatechin, have been shown to inhibit the buildup of tau protein in the brain, a hallmark of Alzheimer's disease. Cinnamon may also improve brain function and protect against neurodegenerative diseases.

Disease Prevention

  • Cinnamon's ability to lower blood sugar levels and improve insulin sensitivity makes it a valuable supplement for managing diabetes. Regular consumption can help stabilize blood sugar levels and reduce the risk of complications associated with diabetes.
  • The antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties of cinnamon may help protect against cancer by reducing oxidative stress and inhibiting the growth of cancer cells. Some studies indicate that cinnamon extracts can reduce the proliferation of cancer cells and induce cell death.
  • Cinnamon's antimicrobial properties can help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections, such as the common cold and bronchitis. It can soothe sore throats and reduce coughs.
