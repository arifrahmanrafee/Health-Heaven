China Rose

Scientic name : Hibiscus rosa-sinensis

H. rosa-sinensis (family: Malvaceae) is locally known as ‘joba’ in Bangladesh and commonly known as ‘China rose’ worldwide. The root, leaf, and ower of H. rosa-sinensis contains different active constit-uents such as tannins, steroids, anthraquinones, essential oils, quinines, mucilage, phenols, reducing sugars, avonoids, carbohydrates, free amino acids, alkaloids, proteins, terpenoids, cardiac glycosides, and saponins. Traditionally, the root of the plant is used for treating ulcers. The administration of aque-ous ower extract from this plant (250 mg/kg) revealed gastric ulcer suppressive activity against pylo-rus-ligation, aspirin-induced, and ethanolinduced ulcers in vivo and this protective activity occurred due to the presence of avonoids and tannins as free radical quenchers . Fruits are used externally to cure sprains, wounds, and ulcers.

Health Benefits

  • Hibiscus tea, made from the flowers of Hibiscus rosa-sinensis, is known to help lower blood pressure and cholesterol levels. This can reduce the risk of cardiovascular diseases, such as heart attacks and strokes.
  • Drinking hibiscus tea can help with weight management by reducing fat absorption and enhancing metabolism. It is also a natural diuretic, which helps reduce water retention.
  • Hibiscus is known to have emmenagogue properties, which means it can help regulate menstrual cycles and alleviate menstrual pain and cramps.
  • The antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties of Hibiscus rosa-sinensis can help improve skin health. The plant is often used in skincare products to promote a clear complexion, reduce acne, and slow down the aging process.

Disease Prevention

  • Hibiscus tea has been shown to help lower blood sugar levels, making it beneficial for people with diabetes. It also helps improve insulin sensitivity and reduces the risk of diabetes-related complications.
  • The hepatoprotective properties of hibiscus can help protect the liver from damage and improve its function. Regular consumption of hibiscus tea can aid in detoxification and promote liver health.
  • The anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties of Hibiscus rosa-sinensis can help alleviate respiratory conditions like cough, colds, and bronchitis. The plant's soothing effects can reduce throat irritation and promote easier breathing.
